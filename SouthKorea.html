Introduction

South Korea is the capital of K-pop as we all know, but it is not just that. South Korea is a country with a fascinating culture. For a long time, South Korea lived in the shadow of its more powerful and well-known neighbours, namely Japan and China. South Korea was like the middle child of Asia: unknown and ignored at best or just seen an ersatz of Japan at worst.

I feel like I have personal relationship with South Korea and the Korean peninsula in general. I practised Taekwondo, the national martial arts, from age 7 to 10 and this is how I learned to count from 1 to 10 in Korean (stay tuned as I’ll give you more language tips in this article). During my second and third year of university (2011-2012) I met awesome Korean people that I now consider my friends. So naturally, in 2015, I decided to visit South Korea and it was one of my best trips ever. In this article I’ll give you some tips to successfully discover Korea.

Are you ready?

What to see?

I'm going to focus on 3 cities: Seoul, Busan and Jeju island.

Seoul is the capital of South Korea and this is where most tourists get in the country via the Incheon International airport. There are lots of things to do in Seoul but in my opinion, you shouldn’t miss:

  • Gyeongbokgung Park
  • Gwanghwamun Square
  • Lotte World
  • Bukhansan National Park
  • Gangnam

Food

Korea has a rich gastronomy and I was absolutely delighted with the food there. Quality restaurants are plentiful, and they are very famous for their "BBQ" style tables. I don’t have any addresses in mind but if you go to Korea you should absolutely try the following dishes:

  • Kimchi
  • Bibimbap (mixed rice)
  • Bulgogi (marinated beef barbecue)

Transport

Getting to Korea from the UK takes, if you take a direct flight, 11 hours. The main airlines regularly connecting London (any airport) to Seoul (Incheon airport) are Korean Air, British Airways, Finnair, Asiana Airline, Air France, Lufthansa and Etihad Airways. Asiana Airline in my opinion has the best quality / price ratio. My flight was direct and the service on board was excellent!

And then, how do you get and move around in Seoul? No panic! Korea has an excellent public transport system. I was mostly using the subway while I was there. You can buy a T-money card which allows you to use subway and buses. The card itself costs 500 Won (~ £0.33) and the minimum top-up is 2500 Won ( ~ £1,66). The Seoul subway is operational from 5:30 to 24:00 (1 AM, next day). However, some train lines close before the 1 am cut off

Language tips

Korean may seem difficult at first glance, but it is quite easy to learn thanks to its alphabet. The Korean alphabet is called the “Hangul “and has 24 letters. You can learn more about the Korean alphabet here (click here).

For the purpose of this article, I’ll only give you what I call the survival phrases:

  • Hello -> 안녕하세요! -> Ann.yeong.ha.se.yo
  • Goodbye (when leaving) -> 안녕히 계십시오 -> Ann.yeong.hi.gye.sib.si.o
  • Goodbye (when staying) -> 안녕히 가세요! -> Ann.yeong.hi.ga.se.yo
  • Thank you -> 감사합니다 -> Gam.sa.hab.ni.da
  • Yes / No -> 네. / 아니. -> Ne / Ani
  • Sorry -> 미안해요 -> Mian.hae.yo
  • I don’t understand -> 못 알아들었어요-> Mos.al.adeul-eoss.eoyo
  • How much is it? -> 얼마예요? -> Eol.ma.ye.yo?
  • Where are the toilets? -> 화장실이 어디에요 -> Hwa.jang.sil.i.eo.di.eyo?

Counting from 1 to 10:

The Korean counting system is a bit strange because they have two systems:

  • Pure Korean numbers
  • Sino-Korean numbers

Pure Korean numbers:

  1. 하나 -> Hana
  2. 둘 -> Dhul
  3. 셋 -> Sehtt
  4. 넷 -> Nehtt
  5. 다섯-> Da-seot
  6. 여섯 -> Yeo-seot
  7. 일곱 -> Il-gop
  8. 여덟-> Yuh-deol
  9. 아홉 -> Ah-hop
  10. 열 -> Yeol

Sino-Korean numbers:

  1. 일 ->il
  2. 이-> i (pronounced as “e”)
  3. 삼 -> sam
  4. 사 -> sa
  5. 오 -> o
  6. 육 -> yuk
  7. 칠 -> chil
  8. 팔 -> pal
  9. 구-> gu
  10. 십 -> ship

Safety

South Korea has the reputation of being a very safe country and it was my experience as well. I felt very comfortable and safe everywhere I went. Travelling to Korea as a solo female is generally not issue however you should still follow some basic safety rules:

  • Do not give the address of your hotel to a stranger
  • Steer clear of drugs
  • Write down emergency info and keep it in your wallet
  • Lock up your valuable items
